What heirs’ property means

Family land can become hard to manage when ownership is shared or unclear.

Shared family ownership

Heirs’ property often means land passed through a family without a clear title plan. Several relatives may now have interests in the same property.

Warning signs

Tax notices, pressure to sell quickly, missing probate records, or disagreement over who can sign may mean the family needs more information before acting.

What to do now, this week, and this month

Move quickly, but avoid rushed legal decisions.

Today

Pause and save important information

  • Pause before signing anything.
  • Save notices, letters, texts, deed references, and contact names.
  • Write down the deadline, who contacted you, and what they requested.
This week

Gather records and family information

  • Gather the most recent deed and tax notice.
  • List possible heirs and current occupants.
  • Prepare a short summary for support calls.
This month

Talk with qualified help

  • Contact legal aid, a qualified attorney, or a trusted community partner.
  • Bring organized notes or the downloadable brochure to meetings.
  • Confirm county-specific next steps before acting.
