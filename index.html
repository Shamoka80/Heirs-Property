Before you act

Do not sign deeds, sales agreements, tax-sale paperwork, partition documents, or informal family agreements until you understand the legal effect and have had an opportunity to speak with a qualified attorney, legal-aid office, or trusted community organization.

Recommended response sequence

Move quickly, but avoid rushed legal decisions.

Today

Stabilize and preserve information

  • Pause before signing anything.
  • Save notices, letters, texts, deed references, and contact names.
  • Write down the deadline, who contacted you, and what they requested.
This week

Organize records and relationships

  • Gather the most recent deed and tax notice.
  • List possible heirs and current occupants.
  • Prepare a concise one-page summary for support calls.
This month

Confirm options with qualified support

  • Contact legal aid, a qualified attorney, or a trusted community partner.
  • Use the printable guide during meetings.
  • Confirm county-specific next steps before acting.

Need help now?

For heirs’ property assistance in South Carolina, contact the Center for Heirs’ Property at (843) 745-7055 or toll-free at (866) 657-2676. For civil legal-aid intake, contact South Carolina Legal Services at (888) 346-5592.
