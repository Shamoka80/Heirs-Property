First reassurance

Many families face this. You can protect your options by slowing down, gathering records, and getting help before signing anything.

Do these first

  • Keep every letter, notice, and envelope related to the property.
  • Write down who contacted you, what they asked for, and when.
  • Gather deed, tax notice, and probate or estate records.
  • List possible heirs even if you are not fully sure yet.
  • Get legal or community guidance before any signature.

Do not do these things yet

  • Do not sign deeds, contracts, or settlements under pressure.
  • Do not throw away tax or court notices.
  • Do not assume verbal family history is the full legal story.
  • Do not agree to a quick sale before reviewing records.
  • Do not rely on one person’s memory for ownership details.

Do these first today

Pause pressure to sign
Keep all documents together
Gather facts before opinions
Call appropriate help quickly

Need help now?

For heirs’ property assistance in South Carolina, contact the Center for Heirs’ Property at (843) 745-7055 or toll-free at (866) 657-2676. For civil legal-aid intake, contact South Carolina Legal Services at (888) 346-5592.

This guide is educational information, not legal advice. Laws, court procedures, agency rules, and program eligibility can change. Confirm details with an attorney, legal aid office, or official agency before acting.
